3. Coaching Teams

It's not easy to get a team to work to its full potential. Conflicts, power struggles and miscommunication can cause a group to start, stumble and stop.

When your people aren't working together as effectively as they could, team coaching can help. Instead of focusing on individual development, your attention with group coaching is on improving relationships and interactions within the team as a whole.

Team coaching is useful in many situations. For example, it can help people to:

  • Work through interpersonal conflicts
  • Understand their roles within the group
  • Improve relationships and collaboration
  • Align on and achieve team goals

3.4. Team Coaching Processes

Structured coaching models provide frameworks for effective team development.

1. The POSITIVE Model

An 8-step approach for motivating teams toward long-term goals:

  1. Purpose - Define achievement goals
  2. Observations - Assess current situation
  3. SMART - Set specific, measurable goals
  4. Insight - Evaluate goal desirability
  5. Team - Identify support network
  6. Initiate - Plan implementation
  7. Value - Break into milestones
  8. Encourage - Provide ongoing support

2. The PRACTICE Model

A 7-step solution-focused approach:

  1. Identify a Problem
  2. Develop Realistic goals
  3. Generate Alternative solutions
  4. Consider the consequences
  5. Target the most feasible solution
  6. Implement your Chosen solution
  7. Evaluate outcomes

PRO TIP

Use the PRACTICE model for specific team challenges and the POSITIVE model for long-term goal achievement.
